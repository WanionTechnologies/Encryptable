# Who Would Value This Framework

This document describes the types of organizations, teams, and professionals who would benefit from or appreciate the Encryptable Framework.

## Target Audience

### 1. Security-Focused Engineering Teams
- Teams building applications where data privacy and cryptographic isolation are critical.
- Developers working on request-scoped (transient) knowledge systems or cryptographic addressing solutions.

### 2. Compliance-Driven Organizations
- Companies subject to regulations such as HIPAA, PCI-DSS, or GDPR.
- Enterprises needing field-level encryption and auditability for sensitive data.

### 3. SaaS Companies Needing Transient Knowledge Architecture
- Multi-tenant SaaS platforms requiring per-user data isolation and zero-trust security.
- Startups and established vendors offering privacy-first products.

### 4. Principal/Staff Engineers & Security Architects
- Senior technical leaders responsible for designing secure, scalable, and innovative systems.
- Architects evaluating advanced encryption, key management, and data protection strategies.

### 5. Privacy-First Product Teams
- Teams building products where user privacy is a core differentiator.
- Organizations seeking to minimize risk of insider threats and data breaches.

### 6. Regulated Industries
- Healthcare, financial services, government, and defense sectors.
- Any industry where data sovereignty, secure deletion, and cryptographic separation are required.

## Why Value This Framework?
- Advanced cryptographic primitives (AES-256-GCM, HKDF)
- Cryptographic addressing and request-scoped (transient) knowledge architecture
- Field-level encryption and per-user isolation
- Production-ready, extensible, and well-documented
- Suitable for compliance, privacy, and security innovation

## Limitations

Encryptable is intentionally designed with a privacy-first architecture and some unique technical choices. Please review the [Limitations](LIMITATIONS.md) document for important details about supported features, architectural decisions, and integration considerations before adopting the framework.
